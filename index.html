Trip FAQ

How difficult is it, and do I need prior experience?
A moderate fitness level is recommended. Daily hikes range roughly 11–22 km (4–10 hours), with terrain and weather affecting difficulty.
  • Day 1 (Base of the Towers): 22 km, 8–10 hrs, strenuous
  • Day 2: 12 km, 4–5 hrs, moderate
  • Day 3 (French Valley): 17 km, 6–7 hrs, demanding
  • Day 4 (to Grey): 11 km, 4–4.5 hrs, moderate (+3–4 hrs optional suspension bridges)
  • Day 5: 11 km, 4–4.5 hrs back to Paine Grande (or do the bridges in the morning, then return later)
The program isn't suitable for children under 10. Patagonia weather changes quickly — pack layers and wind protection.
What does "self-guided" mean, and who is it best for?
You hike independently on well-marked trails while the outfitter handles the logistics — transportation, permits, catamaran tickets, meals, and refugio or camping reservations. You'll receive maps and a pre-trip briefing in Puerto Natales, and you'll likely meet many other trekkers along the way. It's ideal for travelers who want flexible pacing, social hut-to-hut overnights, and comprehensive support without a guide.
Do I need to carry my own gear?
Yes — on the self-guided 5-day W Trek you'll carry your personal items for the day (water, clothing, snacks, camera, etc.). A porter option is available if you'd rather hike with just a daypack. Here's a solid packing list guide to start from.
